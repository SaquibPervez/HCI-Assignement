Understanding Interaction Design

What is Interaction Design?

Interaction design focuses on creating interactive products that are usable. Usability involves being easy to learn, effective to use, and providing an enjoyable user experience.

Designers identify weaknesses and strengths of different interactive systems to create usable products.

What to Design?

Key considerations include understanding who will use the product, where it will be used, and the kind of activities it supports. For example, systems for remote communication should enable easy input and interaction with messages.

Designers face diverse options, from multimedia applications to virtual reality environments and speech-based systems.

Goals of Interaction Design

Usability goals focus on efficiency, safety, and learnability, while user experience goals aim to make the interaction satisfying, enjoyable, and aesthetically pleasing.

The Design Process

The process involves identifying needs, developing alternatives, building interactive versions, and evaluating them iteratively with a user-centered approach.
